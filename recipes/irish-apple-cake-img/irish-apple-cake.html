Irish Apple Cake

Home

Description

This Irish apple cake has a delicate texture and is topped with a cinnamon-oat streusel and cinnamon glaze. A must for St. Patrick's Day or easy enough for an anytime snack.

Ingredients

  • Streusel:

    • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1/2 cup white sugar
    • 1/4 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, cubed

  • Cake:

    • cooking spray
    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
    • 1/4 cup white sugar
    • 1/4 cup packed light brown sugar
    • 2 large eggs, room temperature
    • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
    • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon baking powder
    • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • 3 tablespoons whole milk
    • 2 large Granny Smith apples, peeled and cut into 1/4-inch thick slices

  • Cinnamon Glaze:

    • 1/2 cup confectioners' sugar
    • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 2 teaspoons whole milk

Directions

  1. Step 1

    2. For the streusel, combine flour, sugar, oats, salt, and cinnamon in a bowl. Using a fork or pastry cutter, cut butter into the mixture until it is fully incorporated and has the texture of coarse bread crumbs, about 2 minutes. Chill in the refrigerator until ready to use.

  2. Step 2

    3. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).

  3. Step 3

    4. For the cake, grease a 9-inch round cake pan with cooking spray. Combine butter, sugar, and brown sugar in a medium bowl. Beat at medium speed, until lightened in color and fluffy, 3 to 4 minutes. Beat in eggs, 1 at a time, on low speed, just until blended. Beat in vanilla extract.

  4. Step 4

    5. Combine flour, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt in a small bowl. Add flour mixture to butter-egg mixture and beat on low speed until fully incorporated, 1 to 2 minutes. Add milk and beat on low speed until combined, about 45 seconds.

  5. Step 5

    6. Spread cake batter evenly in the prepared pan.

  6. Step 6

    7. Arrange apple slices evenly on top of batter, overlapping as needed.

  7. Step 7

    8. Sprinkle apples with streusel.

  8. Step 8

    9. Bake in the preheated oven until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean and streusel is golden brown and crisp, 40 to 50 minutes. Let cake cool in pan on a wire rack 10 minutes. Remove from pan to wire rack and let cool completely, 30 to 45 minutes.

  9. Step 9

    10. For the cinnamon glaze, whisk together confectioners' sugar, cinnamon, and milk in a small bowl until smooth.

  10. Step 10

    11. Drizzle glaze over cake before serving.
